# Xiaohei IP

## Character Definition

Xiaohei is the fixed visual IP for Ian's body illustrations.
By default, Xiaohei should appear in every image. Xiaohei is not a mascot, not a sticker, not cute decoration, but an absurd worker earnestly participating in the operation of the system.

## Appearance

- A small solid-black creature.
- White dot eyes.
- Thin legs, occasionally thin arms.
- The body can be a cylinder, black bean, black box, funnel, shadow, hole opening, or a black block inside a machine.
- Slightly irregular outline, with a hand-drawn feel.
- Expression: blank, dazed, calm, serious.

## Personality

- Very earnest, but the things it does are a bit absurd.
- Like a low-key system operator.
- Deadpan humor, not acting cute.
- A bit clumsy, but not stupid.
- Like it's genuinely responsible for some job in a whiteboard sketch.

## Common Duties

Let Xiaohei take on the core action:

- Hauling materials.
- Pulling strings to converge information sources.
- Stuck in a breakpoint.
- Operating a "judgment" lever inside a machine.
- Turning into a filtering funnel.
- Slicing open a "material fish."
- Stamping to endorse messaging.
- Pulling along a continuation path.
- Holding a warning sign looking at a pitfall.
- Reaching out from a hole but unable to catch the content.
- Alongside: hauling bricks, building bridges, opening doors, sorting, recording.

## Prohibited

- Don't draw Xiaohei as an overly cute mascot.
- Don't draw it as a children's cartoon character.
- Don't give Xiaohei complex clothing, meme expressions, or sparkly eyes.
- Don't let Xiaohei just stand in a corner watching.
- Don't let Xiaohei steal the structural expression.
- Don't draw Xiaohei too commercial, too rounded, too polished.

## Judgment Criterion

If you remove Xiaohei and the image's core metaphor still fully holds up, that means Xiaohei was too decorative; rewrite the prompt so that Xiaohei becomes the action subject.
